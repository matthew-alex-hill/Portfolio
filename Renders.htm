Bedroom
Isometric render.
This render displays a bedroom of someone who doesn't believe they deserve luxuries or comfort, supported by gloomy cool lighting which creates a sense of discomfort and sadness in this image. Technically, the render makes use of cloth simulation to produce the bed, pillow and duvet and uses 100% homemade assets. A few of these may be recognisable from my film Sleep, found on the animations page.
Phone
A challenge in modelling.
This render of my phone was made as a modelling and UV editing exercise and taught me about topological correction as well as some texturing techniques like creating multiple UV maps for placing stickers such as the camera texture onto the object. The main challenge I faced creating this phone was adding the charging port and headphone jack which involved having to dissolve down loop cuts using topology correction methods to prevent the placement of the jack affecting the curvature of the shell of the phone.
Cabinet
Creating reusable assets.
This kitchen cabinet was created for a currently unfinished animation; it is one of my assets which I am most proud of. I think this render is the most photorealistic I have ever produced. The main challenge in creating this model was the UV unwrapping and texturing as the main cabinet object has an unusual shape as a box with a hole sunk into the top so it took lots of trial and error to get the seams correct. Texture scaling was another problem as getting the real world scale of all the different textures to match was a real challenge on top of creating the correct patterns in the wood cupboard doors to match my reference.
Subway
Recreating a scene from reference
This render of the Fairfield Subway in Croydon is a recreation of the subway I used to walk through every day on my way home from school. I created it in an attempt to produce a photorealistic scene from a reference image. I used software such as PureRef to inspect the reference and FSpy to match the FOV of the scene to the original photo. The main challenge in creating this pice was painting and blending in surface imperfections like moss, fingerprints, graffiti and smudges. It was difficult to find the realistic balance of how much of each imperfection to add to each surface and where to paint it in.
Pembridge Memories
Character Posing.
This render reflects on my first year in university, showing me and my friends from first year and recreating a picture we took in our halls. The original picture is projected over the characters to match the faces to the characters.  This was my first time creating a character and I did so by using the skin modifier in Blender and by generating my own armature. I'm very happy with how the posing of the characters looks, but I think the posing of hands and fingers could be more natural.
Chairs #1 and #2
From Tutorial
The first chair is taken from an intermediate modelling tutorial by Blender Guru which taught me about edge correction, bevelling, modelling techniques and UV editing. I then transferred these skills into making chair 2 without a tutorial or guide using only reference images taken from an antiques auction site. I'm really pleased with both chairs and how I was able to transfer the skills from creating chair #1 to creating another chair with a completely different design.
